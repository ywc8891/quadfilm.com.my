Revealing the 3 Common Misconceptions Of PPF & Coating
MISCONCEPTION#01:
Not Every Workshop Installed PPF The Right Way
(Resulting Bad Cut, Big Gap, and Peel Off.)
Many workshops provide PPF service, but we never know how well-trained they are.
Some newly opened workshops perhaps only practice PPF installation on just a few cars,
which results in poor execution due to lack of experience.
Proper installation involves advanced techniques,
and only certified installers can ensure professional workmanship.
MISCONCEPTION#02:
Don’t be Deceived by the Thickness of PPF Specification
(Thicker The PPF Doesn’t Mean The Better It Is)
Wrong Way PPF Comparison:
The wrong Question to ask when Choosing PPF is:
“How Thick Is Your PPF Product?”
Thicker of PPF may lead to low tenacity, which causes it harder and difficult to stretch during the installation process.
Worse, when it comes to minor bump or scratch, due to low tenacity it may torn and damage the car body.
Smart Way PPF Comparison:
The right question to ask when comes to PPF comparison is by asking:
“What’s the Material of TPU (Thermal Poly-Urethane)?” &
“What’s the Adhesive of Your PPF?”
The raw material of adhesive and TPU as both are the main element that contribute to the quality of PPF.
Good TPU quality of PPF may Increase the durability from turning yellowish, Prevent PPF become brittle Improve the clarity of PPF over period of time Prevent rapture when handling damages
Whereas a Good PPF Adhesive may Prevent glue residue on car paint after using for 5years or more while prevent edges of PPF peel off from your car body.
Lastly would be the skill of the workshop installers. You can always request photos of their work done (especially PPF edges) or visit the workshop and look at the installation process if they did a neat cutting and debris-free installation.
MISCONCEPTION#03:
Don’t Overspend on Unnecessarily Car Body Protection!
(Full PPF Not Necessarily is the Best Protection)
Paint Protective Film (PPF) is a thick, highly elastic and durable, high performance plastic that resists 95% of physical damage.
It even has the ability to resist physical damage from minor bumps.
Scratches and stone chips are no doubt inevitable
As such it is very important to protect your body paint on the first day you collected the keys to your car.
A Full Car PPF Wrapping Cost RM10k and above,
is it a necessity or is it a luxury?
Many PPF shops will push you to wrap up your whole car with PPF, which can cost from RM10k - RM25k!
But do you really need it?
Yes, if only your car is 200k and above
Your Solution: Full PPF Wrapping
A paint job for luxurious car can cost a minimum of 25k and above
Furthermore if you are planning to sell your car after warranty
It makes more sense to protect your original car painting from day one in order to sell it at a new car condition at 30k more, compare to one with a poor paint condition.
No, if only your car is below 200k
Your Solution: Hybrid PPF Wrapping
if you are driving a CX-5 or at the same segment or below,
We would recommend going for a combination protection with PPF and car coating.
Protect the front of your car (high risk area) where it’s prone to experience physical damages with PPF while other parts with car coating.
That way, you can save at least 50% from Full PPF Wrapping
MISCONCEPTION#04:
Higher the IRR of Tinting Doesn’t Mean the Heat Rejection is Better
(Don’t be deceived by the workshops, here is the truth…)
Almost 90% of Window Film can achieve over 90% IRR, but why do you still feel the heat after installation?
IRR only measures the film’s ability to block infrared rays, it is JUST ONE component of the total heat entering a car.
Full Formula here:
TSER = [ (0.53 x VLT) + (0.44 x IRR) + (0.03 x UVR) ] x Film Material Quality
TSER (Total Solar Energy Rejected) gives a better picture of heat rejection because it measures infrared, visible light, and UV radiation. Relying only on IRR can be MISLEADING since it covers just part of the heat problem.
Some films with high IRR may lose effectiveness after a few months. Ceramic films, which absorb heat, work better in colder climates. But in hot, tropical countries like Malaysia, it’s smarter to use films that reflect heat to keep the interior cool and comfortable.
Choosing the right tint for tropical weather ensures better long-term performance and protection from overheating.
